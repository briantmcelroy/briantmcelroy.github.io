Upcoming Projects

Below are some of the current projects that will be put into repositories in the near future:

  1. University of Arizona's Hearing the Invisible is an art installation to explore the insights that turning brain waves into music can provide. Besides being a production manager and lighting designer for the project, I am also writing a patented protocol for translating EEG data into lighting DMX data. There are multiple difficulties faced in allowing for a visual representation of lighting data, primarily having to do with optimal refresh rate for visual acuity and intensity perception at high frequencies. Because DMX is sampled at 40 Hz and meaningful brain waves go up to 60 Hz or even 120 Hz depending on the region, resampling introduces challenges. It is likely that sampling z-scores of brain wave ranges (delta, gamma, etc.) will result in a far more visually acute effect.

  2. A private medical education company is seeking to predict churn risk and other measures of student success or failure in its student population. It also seeks to aid admissions counselors in directing prospective students towards particular fields based on test scores, personality tests, etc. This is a commissioned work that will have to be carefully anonymized for publishing on my portfolio.
